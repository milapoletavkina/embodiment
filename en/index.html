Philosophy

The body is
the entry point

What we truly feel lives in the body. Embodiment is the union of body and mind, allowing you to experience yourself through somatic experience — not just through thinking.

Stress manifests primarily in the body — through muscle tension, breathing patterns, posture, and fatigue. Working with the body enables genuine change, not just insight.

Embodiment is not an abstract technique; it is a living experience that can be woven into everyday life. It is the HOW of how we inhabit each moment.

About me

Mila
Poletavkina

My professional strength lies in seeing a person in all their multifaceted aspects and choosing embodiment practices for their goals.

In my previous career I worked for 10 years in Moscow as a structural design engineer. Deadlines, burnout, and the feeling of having no inner resources for a full, meaningful life were all very familiar to me.

I know firsthand what it means to move to another country, navigate years of integration into a new culture, and support loved ones through life's most demanding chapters — all of it lived through the body.

My path through general psychology, psychosomatics, and body-based practices led me to embodiment coaching. Every state we experience — sorrow or joy — is directly reflected in the body. Learning how to consciously choose how we live through each experience is a skill we can cultivate, and one that deeply matters.
